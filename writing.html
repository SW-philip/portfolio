Writing

A family argument conducted entirely in verse.

Every April, my dad, my brother, and I try to out-poem each other. We've been at it for most of two decades.

National Poetry Month

April 2026

Quick History

For the better part of this decade, and half of the last one, My father, brother, and I have participated in a sort of round robin poetry battle/contest for the month of April. I've selected a few I've written over the years and put them in the carousel below. Enjoy!

Selected Works:

Introduction (2021)

We thought about it once we
Saw a couple curious moments
Around a very specific date.
But first, we ate, and drank (well she
Did, but not much) and the more we
Thought, the more we thought it
Appropriate to at least take our
Newly shuffled deck, test, and check.

Then that next morning, I sat, oblivious,
Quiet while she, half-nervous/excited
Calibrated in the bath, and upon exiting
Dropped two sticks in my lap that, more
Powerful together, separately concluded
With you, and changed our tiny lives
Forever.

Pouring Sun (2024)

Through vitriol, though such a stupid word,
(Just say the puddled shit between your teeth)
And drool the septic hate until it bursts
The pipe of how we all communicate.
And pad the crumbling holes that were a wall
With cotton, or a cooked incendiary;
A mutual, codependent bleeding out.

Acidic liquid dribbles at my toes
And climbs helictic from my hands and on,
With vagrant hesitance beneath my eyes.
We pour the sun in cracks and hope we glow
In cataclysmic harmony when it ends.
Verity draws the Chekhov in this plot:
We're all some poor cunt's son until we're not.

Le Matin (2025)

It's not so different any other day, well
Anymore. I wake up early, get the dishes
Left from dinner last night where my daughter
Fought me over sweet potato fries. Then try to
Grind the coffee beans and quiet because
My son is sleeping lightly lately in the bassinet
Aside my wife, in bed, and it's for her. I shower
Pulling down the head and seeing faces drawn
In last nights bath of things I've long forgotten,
But am recently starting to picture again.

Then, usually, I'll eat breakfast, but it's harder now
Because the world is lead by troglodytes in
Borrowed skin and fucking wheat and bread, and I
Am gluten-free, and would like to stay not dead.
She'll stir, and it won't matter which i mention
As one begets the other begets the next
Part where I lay my head against my wife.
One simultaneous sigh, and I begin to sizzle
Bacon, maybe, eggs, but lately everyone craves
Pancakes (See?!). She'll ask if we are home today, and
In dismay, she'll tell us that she goes to school on
Tuesday, Wed. and Thursday. Then I'll make her
tie-dyed pancakes, or maybe I'll make Bluey.

I'll make my tea, and as i start to leave, she (trust me
It could be either) neither frowns or smiles, but asks
What Saturday means, and rather than make up
A tiger story or haunted tale, I say "My week is
Done, and I am yours".

Then I'll look for my shoes, and leave, and
Then forget that thing that I forgot, but really,
I can't stand to think of time where any of us
Aren't within a millimeters rule.

Then, as shes gotten fond of recently, she'll turn
To me and ask who I love more. And I will kiss her,
Kiss them both, on lips and head and say, assuredly,
"Imagine you're all swimming, and my love for you's the pool."

Through (2026)

Two trails converged on a grassy tract,
I glanced at both, with time to bide,
And seeing what each grossly lacked,
Was glad I'd entered through the side.

I only guess which way each lead,
From context, maps, and getting lost,
Such crucial things escaped my head;
A fog of warmth in a summer frost.

I don't think I've arrived alone,
There's footprints here that I don't know;
I feel as though my cover's blown.
There're beings hid beneath the snow.

If only I'd a horse, or sled,
Then maybe I'd decide much faster
I'll pace in circles here, instead,
Until I'm caked in coats of plaster.

Two trails converged on a grassy tract,
With time to bide, I glanced at both,
And ignoring what each option lacked,
pushed across it into undergrowth.
