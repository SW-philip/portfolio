System map

How I design conversational AI systems

A framework for building member-facing virtual assistants — followed by two documented flows from a real implementation. The methodology generalizes. The examples are specific.

Framework

The three-entry architecture

Member-facing conversational AI rarely lives in one place. A well-designed system accounts for where the member is when they reach out — what they know, what they can do, and what context the bot has access to.

Public

Unauthenticated. The bot is a concierge — it shows what's possible and routes to login. Context passes forward when the member authenticates.

Authenticated

Full capability. The bot knows the member is logged in and can classify intent, deliver targeted information, and deeplink to the right screen.

Mobile

Same logic as authenticated, different destinations. Mobile apps expose different screens — the conversation is identical, the deeplinks are not.

Principles

What makes a system work

These aren't values statements. They're design decisions that show up in every flow.

01

Build as if I'm the member

Every branch, every response, every question — written from the perspective of a member at 11pm who needs an actual answer. Not a script. Not a policy document.

02

Progressive disambiguation

Don't ask for clarification upfront. Design the first pass to cover the most likely intent, then escalate if the member indicates it didn't solve the problem.

03

No dead ends

A representative is always reachable — through natural language, from anywhere, without a button. "Speak to a representative" routes from every state in the system.

04

API-shaped from the start

Every deeplink is a placeholder for an API call. When integration arrives, you swap the destination — not the conversational logic. The skeleton doesn't change.
